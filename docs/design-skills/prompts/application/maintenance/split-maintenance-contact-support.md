# Split Maintenance Page With Contact Support

Create a maintenance page split into two columns on large 1280px width breakpoints inside a centered page container with comfortable side padding.

**Left column** is a left-aligned vertical stack, vertically centered within the column height, containing in order: a large multi-line heading stating the maintenance status on its own line; a supporting paragraph of one or two sentences directly below the heading at a comfortable reading width, explaining that the site is undergoing planned maintenance and will return soon, containing an inline bold text link pointing to the blog or status page embedded naturally within the sentence; a contact information group placed below the paragraph after extra vertical space, arranged as a horizontal row of two blocks on the same baseline — the first block is a vertical stack composed of a small leading icon paired with a bold label on the same baseline at the top (indicating a physical location or brand entity), followed by a multi-line postal address beneath the label; the second block is a vertical stack composed of a small leading icon paired with a bold label on the same baseline at the top (indicating email contact), followed by a single mailto text link beneath the label; a compact outlined button placed below the contact group on its own line, composed of a small leading chat or message icon paired with a short label (e.g. "Speak with us").

**Right column** holds one large illustrative scene that fills most of the column area, vertically centered within the column. The illustration depicts a figure sitting on or beside server hardware or storage boxes, working at a laptop, conveying a calm maintenance-in-progress theme without relying on photographic imagery.

On narrow viewports stack the two columns vertically with the content column first and the illustration directly below it; keep all internal stacks in their original reading order with comfortable side padding.
