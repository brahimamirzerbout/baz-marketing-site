# Full-Viewport Application Shell With Dual Rails

Create an application shell composed of a fixed top navbar spanning the full viewport width, a narrow icon-only rail on the far left edge, an expanded text sidebar panel adjacent to the icon rail, the main content area in the center, and a narrow icon-only rail on the far right edge.

**Top navbar** is pinned to the top of the viewport as a full-width horizontal bar spanning above all columns. From left to right on the same baseline: a hamburger menu icon; a brand mark composed of a small logo icon paired with the brand name to its right; a search input field with a leading search icon and placeholder text; then grouped on the right end of the bar, a compact primary action button composed of a small leading plus icon and a short label, a small icon-only theme toggle button, a small icon-only notification bell button, a small circular user avatar, and a text logout link.

**Left icon rail** is a narrow fixed-width vertical strip anchored to the far left edge of the viewport below the navbar, stretching from the bottom of the navbar to the bottom of the viewport. It contains a vertical stack of small icon-only buttons, each representing a top-level navigation category, centered horizontally in the strip with consistent vertical spacing between them.

**Expanded sidebar panel** sits immediately to the right of the icon rail as a wider fixed-width vertical panel stretching from the bottom of the navbar to the bottom of the viewport. At the top of the panel, place a header row composed of a short title label on the left and a compact icon-only add button on the right on the same baseline. Below the header, place a search input field with a leading search icon and placeholder text spanning the full panel width. Below the search field, stack a vertical navigation list. Each primary navigation item is a horizontal row composed of a text label on the left and a trailing chevron icon on the right. One section carries a highlight state (active color) and is expanded to show indented child links below the parent. One item includes a small colored numeric badge to the right of the label. At the very bottom of the panel, anchor a user identity block composed of a small circular avatar on the left, a two-line text group (user name on the first line and email on the second line) to the right of the avatar, and a small trailing chevron or caret icon.

**Right icon rail** is a narrow fixed-width vertical strip anchored to the far right edge of the viewport below the navbar, mirroring the left icon rail in width and structure. It contains a vertical stack of small icon-only buttons centered horizontally with consistent vertical spacing.

**Main content area** fills the remaining horizontal space between the expanded sidebar panel and the right icon rail, and vertically from the bottom of the navbar to the bottom of the viewport. Content placeholder regions are arranged as a dashboard grid: a top row of three equal-width regions, a middle row of three equal-width regions, and a full-width region at the bottom.

**Sizing rules.** The shell occupies the full viewport: it spans the viewport width edge to edge and stretches from the very top to the very bottom of the viewport with no surrounding page padding, no outer rounded frame, and no enclosing max-width container around the shell itself. Every dotted (dashed-outline) placeholder region inside the main content area is a short horizontal block approximately 240 pixels tall and never grows to absorb remaining vertical space; empty space below the last placeholder remains visible as the content-area background. Every visible action button inside the shell (primary, secondary outlined, dashed, full-width submit, and any inline composite action button with a label) shares one sizing rule: 12 pixels of horizontal padding on each side, 6 pixels of vertical padding on top and bottom, label text at 12 pixels in a medium font weight, and any small leading or trailing icon aligned to the label's vertical center. This button sizing rule does not apply to icon-only utility buttons, to text-only navigation links or tab triggers, or to user-identity buttons. Colors, borders, surfaces, and hover or focus states stay agnostic and follow the host design system.

On narrow viewports both icon rails and the expanded sidebar panel collapse off-screen. The sidebar becomes accessible via the hamburger icon. The main content area expands to the full viewport width. Grid columns stack vertically in reading order.
