# Two-Bar Application Shell With Sidebar Card

Create an application shell composed of two fixed horizontal bars stacked at the top of the viewport and a left sidebar card panel below them, with the main content area filling the remaining space to the right of the sidebar and below the top bars.

**Top utility bar** is pinned to the very top of the viewport as a slim full-width horizontal strip. On the left side, place a horizontal row of contact details sitting side by side on the same baseline (a small phone icon paired with a phone number, and a small building/company icon paired with an email address). On the right side of the same strip, place a row of controls: a small flag icon paired with a language label and a trailing dropdown chevron, followed by a horizontal row of small social-media icon links sitting side by side on the same baseline, and a text login link at the far right.

**Primary navbar** sits directly below the utility bar as a full-width horizontal bar. On the left side, place a pair of compact secondary button controls (such as an "Open app" button and a "More" dropdown). In the center, place a brand mark composed of a small logo icon paired with the brand name to its right. On the right side, place a horizontal row of utility controls on the same baseline: a small icon-only grid toggle, a small icon-only notification bell, a region selector composed of a location label and a trailing dropdown chevron, and a user identity block composed of a small circular avatar, the user's name, a role badge in parentheses, and a trailing dropdown chevron.

**Left sidebar** is rendered as a distinct card panel with rounded corners and a subtle elevated treatment, positioned below the primary navbar on the left side of the viewport with comfortable margin from the top and left edges. At the top of the card, place a user identity block composed of a small circular avatar on the left, a two-line text group (user name on the first line and email on the second line) to the right of the avatar, and a small trailing sort or swap icon. Below the user block, stack a vertical navigation list of icon-and-label items with one item carrying an active highlight state. Below the main navigation, separate a titled section (such as "My projects") with a bold heading on its own line followed by a vertical list of project items, each composed of a small colored dot indicator on the left and a project name label to its right. Below the project list, place one full-width secondary button composed of a small leading plus icon and a short "Add new project" label. At the very bottom of the card, anchor a group of utility links (such as Settings, Help & Terms, Invite teammates), each composed of a small leading icon and a text label.

**Main content area** fills the remaining space to the right of the sidebar card and below the primary navbar. Content placeholder regions are arranged as a dashboard grid: a full-width region at the top, a row of three equal-width regions, a row of two unequal-width regions (wider left, narrower right), a second row of three equal-width smaller regions, and a full-width region at the bottom.

**Sizing rules.** The shell occupies the full viewport: it spans the viewport width edge to edge and stretches from the very top to the very bottom of the viewport with no surrounding page padding, no outer rounded frame, and no enclosing max-width container around the shell itself. Every dotted (dashed-outline) placeholder region inside the main content area is a short horizontal block approximately 240 pixels tall and never grows to absorb remaining vertical space; empty space below the last placeholder remains visible as the content-area background. Every visible action button inside the shell (primary, secondary outlined, dashed, full-width submit, and any inline composite action button with a label) shares one sizing rule: 12 pixels of horizontal padding on each side, 6 pixels of vertical padding on top and bottom, label text at 12 pixels in a medium font weight, and any small leading or trailing icon aligned to the label's vertical center. This button sizing rule does not apply to icon-only utility buttons, to text-only navigation links or tab triggers, or to user-identity buttons. Colors, borders, surfaces, and hover or focus states stay agnostic and follow the host design system.

On narrow viewports the sidebar card collapses off-screen and becomes accessible via a hamburger or toggle. The top utility bar may condense or hide behind a toggle. The primary navbar compresses to essential elements with the brand mark remaining centered. Grid columns stack vertically in reading order.
