# Three-Bar Application Shell With Two-Column Content

Create an application shell composed of three fixed horizontal bars stacked at the top of the viewport, with the remaining vertical space below given entirely to the main content area. There is no sidebar in this layout.

**Announcement bar** is pinned to the very top of the viewport as a slim full-width horizontal strip. On the left side, place a horizontal row of short key-value metric pairs sitting side by side on the same baseline with consistent spacing (each pair is composed of a short label followed by a prominently styled value). On the right side of the same strip, place a locale and currency selector row: a small flag icon paired with a language label and a trailing dropdown chevron, followed by a currency code with a trailing dropdown chevron.

**Primary navbar** sits directly below the announcement bar as a full-width horizontal bar. From left to right on the same baseline: a hamburger menu icon; a brand mark composed of a small logo icon paired with the brand name to its right; a search input field with a leading search icon and placeholder text; then grouped on the right end of the bar, a compact primary action button composed of a small leading plus icon and a short label, a small icon-only theme toggle button, a small icon-only notification bell button, a small circular user avatar, and a text logout link.

**Breadcrumb and actions bar** sits directly below the primary navbar as a full-width horizontal bar with a thin border or subtle background shift to visually separate it. On the left side, place a breadcrumb row composed of icon-and-label segments separated by chevron or arrow dividers, where each segment may include a trailing dropdown chevron. On the right side of the same bar, place a row of action controls: a secondary button composed of a small leading icon and a short label, followed by a primary action button composed of a short label and a trailing plus icon.

**Main content area** fills the remaining viewport height below the breadcrumb bar. Content placeholder regions are arranged as two equal-width columns sitting side by side inside a centered max-width container with comfortable padding.

**Sizing rules.** The shell occupies the full viewport: it spans the viewport width edge to edge and stretches from the very top to the very bottom of the viewport with no surrounding page padding, no outer rounded frame, and no enclosing max-width container around the shell itself. Each of the two dotted (dashed-outline) placeholder columns inside the main content area is a tall vertical block approximately 800 pixels tall — taller than every other variant in this set — and never grows to absorb remaining vertical space; the two columns sit side by side at the top of the content area and any empty space below them remains visible as the content-area background, scrolling vertically if the placeholder height exceeds the available viewport room. Every visible action button inside the shell (primary, secondary outlined, dashed, full-width submit, and any inline composite action button with a label) shares one sizing rule: 12 pixels of horizontal padding on each side, 6 pixels of vertical padding on top and bottom, label text at 12 pixels in a medium font weight, and any small leading or trailing icon aligned to the label's vertical center. This button sizing rule does not apply to icon-only utility buttons, to text-only navigation links or tab triggers, or to user-identity buttons. Colors, borders, surfaces, and hover or focus states stay agnostic and follow the host design system.

On narrow viewports the announcement bar may collapse into a horizontally scrollable strip or hide behind a toggle. The primary navbar compresses to essential elements. The breadcrumb bar may truncate to the last segment with a back arrow. The two-column layout stacks vertically in reading order.
