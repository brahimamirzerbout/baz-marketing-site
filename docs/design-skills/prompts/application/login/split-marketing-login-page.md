# Split Marketing Login Page

Create a login page split into two columns on large 1280px width breakpoints inside a centered page container with comfortable side padding. The left column is a marketing column carrying copy and a short feature list; the right column is the form column. The two columns share the same vertical center line.

The **left column** is a left-aligned vertical stack containing, in order: a large multi-line heading on its own line; a supporting paragraph below the heading at a comfortable reading width; a vertical list of about three feature rows each composed of a small leading icon at the left, a bold one-line title to the right of the icon on the same baseline, and a short supporting line directly beneath the title that sits flush-left under the icon-and-title row.

The **right column** is a left-aligned vertical stack containing, in order: a heading on its own line; a horizontal row of two equal third-party provider buttons sitting side by side (each button carries a small leading provider glyph and a short provider label); a horizontal "or" separator made of a thin rule, a centered short word, and another thin rule; a two-column field grid containing two labeled inputs on one row (email input on the left and password input on the right) — every label sits above its input with a tiny required-field marker to the right of the label text; a single horizontal row composed of a small square "remember me" checkbox paired with an inline label on the left and a "forgot password" text link on the right; one full-width primary submit button beneath the checkbox row; finally a small helper line containing a short prompt sentence followed by an inline text link to the sign-up flow.

On narrow viewports, stack with the form column first and the marketing column second so the user reaches the form quickly; keep all internal stacks in their original reading order.
