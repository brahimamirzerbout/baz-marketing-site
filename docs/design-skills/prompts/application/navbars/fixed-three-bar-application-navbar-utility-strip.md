# Three-Bar Application Navbar With Utility Strip

Create an application navbar composed of three fixed horizontal bars stacked at the top of the viewport, all spanning the full viewport width edge to edge with no surrounding page padding and no enclosing max-width container around the bars themselves.

**Top utility bar** is pinned to the very top of the viewport as a slim full-width horizontal strip. On the left side, place a rating group composed of a horizontal row of small star icons sitting side by side on the same baseline followed by a small pill-shaped badge containing a short rating value (such as "4.8 out of 5"). On the right side of the same strip, place a horizontal row of short trust or service highlights sitting side by side on the same baseline with consistent spacing between them; each highlight is composed of a small leading icon paired with a short text label to its right on the same baseline (such as "Fast & Free delivery", "Fair prices", "Support 24/7").

**Primary navbar** sits directly below the top utility bar as a full-width horizontal bar. From left to right on the same baseline: a hamburger menu icon; a brand mark composed of a small logo icon paired with the brand name to its right; a search input field with a leading search icon and placeholder text, sized to occupy a moderate portion of the bar's width directly after the brand mark; then grouped on the right end of the bar, a row of small icon-only utility buttons sitting side by side on the same baseline (such as a theme toggle, a settings gear, and a grid-view toggle), followed by a compact primary action button composed of a small leading plus icon and a short label, and finally a user identity block composed of a small circular avatar paired with the user's name and a trailing dropdown chevron.

**Secondary navigation bar** sits directly below the primary navbar as a full-width horizontal bar with a thin top border to separate it from the primary navbar. On the left side of the bar, place a horizontal row of text navigation links sitting side by side on the same baseline with consistent spacing between them. Some entries include a trailing chevron icon indicating a dropdown submenu, and the row ends with a small overflow icon for additional links.

**Sizing rules.** The navbar spans the full viewport width edge to edge with no surrounding page padding and no enclosing max-width container around the bars themselves. Do not generate any main content area or placeholder blocks below the navbar; only output the navbar code. Every visible action button inside the navbar (primary, secondary outlined, dashed, and any inline composite action button with a label) shares one sizing rule: 12 pixels of horizontal padding on each side, 6 pixels of vertical padding on top and bottom, label text at 12 pixels in a medium font weight, and any small leading or trailing icon aligned to the label's vertical center. This button sizing rule does not apply to icon-only utility buttons, to text-only navigation links, or to user-identity buttons. Colors, borders, surfaces, and hover or focus states stay agnostic and follow the host design system.

On narrow viewports the top utility bar may condense into a horizontally scrollable strip or hide behind a toggle (the rating group and trust highlights stack or wrap); the primary navbar compresses to essential elements (search collapses behind a search icon, utility icons group behind an overflow menu, primary action becomes icon-only, user block reduces to avatar-only); the secondary navigation bar collapses into the hamburger menu or becomes a horizontally scrollable strip with the active link scrolled into view. Preserve reading order throughout.
