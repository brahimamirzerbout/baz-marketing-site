# Three-Bar Application Navbar

Create an application navbar composed of three fixed horizontal bars stacked at the top of the viewport, all spanning the full viewport width edge to edge with no surrounding page padding and no enclosing max-width container around the bars themselves.

**Announcement banner** is pinned to the very top of the viewport as a slim full-width horizontal strip with a subtly different background to separate it from the navbar below. Inside the strip, center a single inline message group on the same baseline composed of a small leading info or megaphone icon, a short multi-word announcement message directly after the icon, and a trailing inline link composed of a short labeled call-to-action (such as "Check it out") with a small trailing arrow icon.

**Primary navbar** sits directly below the announcement banner as a full-width horizontal bar. From left to right on the same baseline: a hamburger menu icon; a brand mark composed of a small logo icon paired with the brand name to its right; then grouped on the right end of the bar, a small icon-only grid-view toggle, a small icon-only notification bell, a compact search trigger composed of a small leading search icon paired with a short "Search" label, a region selector composed of a small flag or location glyph paired with a location label and a trailing dropdown chevron, and finally a user identity block composed of a small circular avatar paired with the user's name and a small role badge in parentheses.

**Editor action toolbar** sits directly below the primary navbar as a full-width horizontal bar with a thin top border to separate it from the primary navbar. On the left side of the bar, place a horizontal row of editor controls sitting side by side on the same baseline: a secondary outlined button composed of a small leading reset or refresh icon paired with a short label (such as "Reset"), followed by a pair of small icon-only history controls (undo and redo), followed by a small leading clock icon paired with a short status line of plain text describing the last update. On the right side of the same bar, place a row of action controls on the same baseline: a secondary outlined button composed of a small leading calendar icon paired with a short label (such as "Schedule task"), followed by a compact primary action button composed of a short label and a trailing plus icon (such as "New task").

**Sizing rules.** The navbar spans the full viewport width edge to edge with no surrounding page padding and no enclosing max-width container around the bars themselves. Do not generate any main content area or placeholder blocks below the navbar; only output the navbar code. Every visible action button inside the navbar (primary, secondary outlined, dashed, and any inline composite action button with a label) shares one sizing rule: 12 pixels of horizontal padding on each side, 6 pixels of vertical padding on top and bottom, label text at 12 pixels in a medium font weight, and any small leading or trailing icon aligned to the label's vertical center. This button sizing rule does not apply to icon-only utility buttons, to text-only navigation links, or to user-identity buttons. Colors, borders, surfaces, and hover or focus states stay agnostic and follow the host design system.

On narrow viewports the announcement banner may truncate its message or hide behind a dismiss toggle; the primary navbar compresses to essential elements (search trigger and region selector collapse behind icons, user block reduces to avatar-only); the editor action toolbar stacks into two rows or collapses into an overflow menu with the status line truncating. Preserve reading order throughout.
