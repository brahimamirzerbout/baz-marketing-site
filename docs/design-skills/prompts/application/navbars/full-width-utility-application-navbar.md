# Full-Width Utility Application Navbar

Create an application navbar composed of two fixed horizontal bars stacked at the top of the viewport, both spanning the full viewport width edge to edge with no surrounding page padding and no enclosing max-width container around the bars themselves.

**Top utility bar** is pinned to the very top of the viewport as a slim full-width horizontal strip. On the left side, place a horizontal row of contact details sitting side by side on the same baseline (a small phone icon paired with a phone number, and a small email or company icon paired with an email address). On the right side of the same strip, place a row of controls on the same baseline: a small flag icon paired with a language label and a trailing dropdown chevron, followed by a horizontal row of small social-media icon links sitting side by side with consistent spacing between them, and a text login link with a small leading or trailing arrow icon at the far right.

**Primary navbar** sits directly below the top utility bar as a full-width horizontal bar. The bar is divided into three regions on the same baseline. On the left side, place a pair of compact secondary button controls sitting side by side (such as a labeled "Open app" button followed by a labeled "More" dropdown button with a trailing up-down caret icon). In the horizontal center of the bar, place a brand mark composed of a small logo icon paired with the brand name to its right on the same baseline. On the right side, place a horizontal row of utility controls on the same baseline: a small icon-only grid toggle, a small icon-only notification bell, a region selector composed of a location label and a trailing dropdown chevron, and a user identity block composed of a small circular avatar paired with the user's name and a small role badge in parentheses.

**Sizing rules.** The navbar spans the full viewport width edge to edge with no surrounding page padding and no enclosing max-width container around the bars themselves. Do not generate any main content area or placeholder blocks below the navbar; only output the navbar code. Every visible action button inside the navbar (primary, secondary outlined, dashed, and any inline composite action button with a label) shares one sizing rule: 12 pixels of horizontal padding on each side, 6 pixels of vertical padding on top and bottom, label text at 12 pixels in a medium font weight, and any small leading or trailing icon aligned to the label's vertical center. This button sizing rule does not apply to icon-only utility buttons, to text-only navigation links, or to user-identity buttons. Colors, borders, surfaces, and hover or focus states stay agnostic and follow the host design system.

On narrow viewports the top utility bar may condense or hide behind a toggle; the primary navbar compresses to essential elements with the brand mark remaining visible; the left button pair and right utility group collapse behind a menu toggle; the user block reduces to avatar-only. Preserve reading order throughout.
