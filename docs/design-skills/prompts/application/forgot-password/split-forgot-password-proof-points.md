# Split Forgot Password With Proof Points

Create a forgot-password page split into two columns on large 1280px width breakpoints inside a centered page container with comfortable side padding. The two columns are separated by a thin vertical border running the full height of the section.

**Left column** is a left-aligned vertical stack, vertically centered within the column height, containing in order: a large bold heading stating a trust metric on its own line (e.g. "Over 200,000+ happy clients"); a supporting paragraph of one or two sentences directly below the heading at a comfortable reading width, describing the platform's value proposition for teams; a vertical list of three proof points placed below the paragraph after extra vertical space, each proof point is a horizontal row composed of a small leading icon on the left and a vertical stack to the right of the icon containing a bold metric label on its own line (e.g. "35+ finished projects") and a short supporting sentence directly below the label.

**Right column** is a left-aligned vertical stack, vertically centered within the column height, containing a compact reset-password form. Stack from top to bottom: a bold heading on its own line (e.g. "Forgot your password?"); a supporting paragraph of one or two sentences directly below the heading at a comfortable reading width, reassuring the user and explaining the reset flow; a labeled email input row placed below the paragraph (the label sits above the input on its own line with a tiny required-field marker to the right of the label text; the input carries a leading envelope icon affordance flush-left inside the field and a placeholder suggesting an email address); a single checkbox row containing a small square checkbox at the left and an inline label whose text confirms acceptance of linked terms and conditions (with the terms phrase rendered as an inline text link); one full-width primary action button at the bottom with a short label (e.g. "Reset password").

On narrow viewports stack the two columns vertically with the social proof column first and the form column directly below it; keep all internal stacks in their original reading order with comfortable side padding.
