# Full-Width Split Forgot Password Page

Create a forgot-password page split into two columns on large 1280px width breakpoints that spans the full available width of the viewport with no outer margin.

**Left column** uses a plain page background and contains a left-aligned vertical stack constrained to a 424px max width, vertically and horizontally centered within the column. Stack from top to bottom: a bold heading on its own line (e.g. "Forgot your password?"); a supporting paragraph of one or two sentences directly below the heading at a comfortable reading width, reassuring the user and explaining the reset flow; a labeled email input row placed below the paragraph after extra vertical space (the label sits above the input on its own line with a tiny required-field marker to the right of the label text; the input carries a leading envelope icon affordance flush-left inside the field and a placeholder suggesting an email address); a single checkbox row containing a small square checkbox at the left and an inline label whose text confirms acceptance of linked terms and conditions (with the terms phrase rendered as an inline text link); one full-width primary action button below the checkbox with a short label (e.g. "Reset password").

**Right column** uses a distinct filled background that spans the full column area edge-to-edge. Inside the column, place a centered vertical stack vertically centered within the column height containing: a small brand icon or logo mark on its own line paired with a brand name label immediately to the right of the icon on the same baseline; a large bold heading on its own line directly below the brand row delivering a compelling value statement (e.g. "Explore the world's leading design portfolios."); a supporting paragraph of one or two sentences directly below the heading at a comfortable reading width describing the platform community; a social proof row placed below the paragraph, composed of slightly overlapping circular user avatars on the left and a short line of text immediately to the right of the avatars on the same baseline stating a review metric (e.g. "Rated best over 15.7k reviews").

On narrow viewports stack the two columns vertically with the form column first and the branded column directly below it; each internal stack preserves its original reading order with comfortable side padding.
