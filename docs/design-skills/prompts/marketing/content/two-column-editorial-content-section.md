# Two-Column Editorial Content Section

Create a content section split into two columns on large 1280px width breakpoints inside a centered page container with comfortable side padding. The two columns share the same vertical center line.

The **left column** is a left-aligned vertical stack containing, in order: a large heading on its own line; three supporting paragraphs arranged one after another directly beneath the heading (each paragraph separated by consistent vertical spacing); then a horizontal row of two CTA buttons sitting side by side (a secondary button whose label pairs with a small leading book or document icon at the left end, and a primary button whose label pairs with a small trailing arrow icon at the right end). Keep the entire text-and-button stack at a comfortable reading width within the column.

The **right column** contains one large illustrative scene or image that fills the column width and stretches to match the height of the left column's content. Vertically center the illustration relative to the left column so both columns share the same visual midpoint.

On narrow viewports, stack the text-and-buttons column above the illustration; the illustration may scale down proportionally to fit the single-column width.
