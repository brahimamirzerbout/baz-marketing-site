# Narrative Header With Three-Card Grid

Create a content section as a vertical stack inside a centered page container with comfortable side padding.

The **section label** sits at the top left. Place a small typographic label (for example a short slash-prefixed word) at the top-left corner or flush-left above the heading so it reads as a category marker.

The **narrative header** sits below the section label. Stack a large multi-line heading on its own line and a supporting paragraph directly beneath the heading at a comfortable reading width, both left-aligned.

The **card grid** sits beneath the narrative header as three equal-width columns on large 1280px width breakpoints with consistent gutters between them.

The **first card** (left column) is a media-forward card: a background image or texture fills the card area; a short title is placed toward the top of the card (left-aligned with comfortable inset padding); and a longer caption paragraph is anchored toward the bottom of the card (left-aligned with comfortable inset padding). The card carries rounded corners.

The **second card** (center column) follows the same media-forward structure as the first card: background image or texture filling the area, a short title toward the top, and a caption paragraph anchored toward the bottom, with the same rounded corners and inset padding.

The **third card** (right column) is a solid contrast panel with rounded corners: it stacks a short prompt heading and one primary button (centered or left-aligned within the panel) so the panel reads as a call-to-action tile rather than a content card.

On narrow viewports, stack the three columns vertically with consistent spacing between them; keep each card's internal layout intact.
