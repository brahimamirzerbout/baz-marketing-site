# Split Content With Video CTA

Create a content section split into two columns on large 1280px width breakpoints inside a centered page container with comfortable side padding. The two columns share the same vertical center line.

The **left column** is a left-aligned vertical stack containing, in order: a large multi-line heading on its own line; a supporting paragraph directly beneath the heading at a comfortable reading width; then a horizontal row of two CTA buttons sitting side by side (a primary button whose label pairs with a small trailing arrow icon at the right end of the label, and a secondary button whose label pairs with a small leading eye icon at the left end of the label). Keep consistent vertical spacing between the heading, paragraph, and button row.

The **right column** contains one large rectangular media block sized to a 16 : 9 video-frame aspect ratio that fills the column width. Center a circular play-button control on top of the media block (the control sits at the exact horizontal and vertical center of the rectangle and carries a small triangular play glyph inside it). The media block may carry a placeholder background image or a solid filled surface behind the play control.

On narrow viewports, stack the two columns vertically with the text-and-buttons column first and the media block beneath it; keep all internal stacks in their original reading order.
