# Tabbed FAQ Accordion Section

Create a FAQ section inside a centered page container with comfortable side padding. At the top, place a left-aligned header block composed of a large left-aligned heading on its own line followed by a supporting paragraph directly beneath the heading at a comfortable reading width.

Below the header, split the section into two columns on large breakpoints.

The **left column** is a vertical navigation list of category labels that behaves as a tab group (exactly one category is active at a time). Each label is a horizontal row composed of a small icon anchored to the left and a short category name to its right on the same baseline. The active label carries a distinct visual indicator (such as a stronger weight or an accent side marker) to distinguish it from inactive labels.

The **right column** displays the accordion items belonging to the currently active category. Stack the items vertically with consistent spacing between them. Each item follows the same collapsible pattern: a horizontal row composed of a small question-mark icon on the left, a bold question label to the right of the icon on the same baseline, and a small chevron icon anchored to the far right of the row. When expanded, the chevron rotates and a content region appears directly below the question row containing one or more paragraphs of answer text at a comfortable reading width; inline text links may appear within the answer copy.

Below the two-column block, place a full-width footer bar separated by a thin horizontal rule. The bar is a horizontal row containing a short support prompt on the left and a compact button on the right on the same baseline, with the button label preceded by a small leading icon.

On narrow viewports stack the category navigation above the accordion panel in reading order; the footer bar elements stack vertically and center.
