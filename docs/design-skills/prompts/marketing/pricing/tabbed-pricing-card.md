# Tabbed Pricing Card

Create a pricing section as one vertically stacked block inside a max-width container. At the top, place a centered heading on its own line; directly below the heading, a centered supporting paragraph at a comfortable reading width.

Directly below the paragraph, place a horizontal row of plan tabs centered on the page (one short text label per plan). The active tab is indicated by a short underline flush with the bottom edge of the tab row and stronger typographic emphasis than inactive tabs. Below the tab row, show one large bordered card with rounded corners whose interior content swaps based on the currently active tab.

Inside the card, split into two equal-width columns on large 1280 px width breakpoints.

**Left column** is a padded vertical stack containing, in order: the current plan name on its own line; a very large dominant price on a line directly below the name; a single secondary line of billing-detail copy directly below the price (e.g. "billed annually"); one full-width primary call-to-action button; and directly below it one full-width secondary call-to-action button whose label begins with a small leading icon glyph followed by short action copy.

**Right column** contains a vertical list of feature rows with comfortable vertical spacing; each row is composed of a small circular check icon anchored at the left and a feature label to its right on the same baseline, where inline segments of the label may read as emphasized (e.g. numeric limits or durations called out within the same line).

Below the main card, separated from it by a comfortable gap, place a thin full-width footer bar with rounded corners and a light border. Inside the bar, center a horizontal cluster of two text links separated by a thin vertical divider; each link begins with a small leading icon glyph followed by short label copy.

On narrow viewports the tab row scrolls horizontally or wraps, the card's two columns stack vertically with the pricing column first and the feature list second, and the footer links stack or wrap while staying horizontally centered.
