# Split Benefits and Pricing

Create a pricing section on a flat full-width background inside a max-width container, split into two equal-width columns on large 1280 px width breakpoints with a narrow vertical decorative separator between them (a thin line, hatch, or similar motif — decorative only, not a scrollable panel).

**Left column** is a left-aligned vertical stack containing, in order: a large left-aligned heading on its own line (upgrade or benefits copy, not merely a SKU name); a supporting paragraph directly below the heading at a comfortable reading width; a vertical stack of exactly three feature blocks with comfortable spacing between them — each block is a horizontal row with a small square or squircle icon container on the left (neutral surface, subtle border, simple line icon inside) and, to its right on the same baseline, a bold feature title on its own line with one or two lines of smaller descriptive copy directly beneath the title (not a one-line bullet list). Below the feature stack, after extra vertical space, place a social-proof row: a tight horizontal cluster of overlapping small circular avatar thumbnails on the left and one line of caption copy to their right that includes a numerically emphasized segment (e.g. a user count rendered in bold).

**Right column** is a single pricing surface (an optional very light outer border so it reads as one card) containing a padded vertical stack, in order: a very large dominant price with a small "/ per month" (or "/ month") suffix sitting on the same baseline to the right of the amount; a thin horizontal divider spanning the full surface width directly under the price; a two-column grid of short feature labels (roughly five rows by two columns of items) where each cell is composed of a small circular check icon at the left and a short label to its right on the same baseline; and a horizontal row of two buttons at the bottom — a secondary outlined or soft button on the left (e.g. a free- trial action) and a solid primary button on the right whose label includes a trailing arrow or chevron glyph.

On narrow viewports the two columns stack vertically with the left column first and the pricing surface second; each internal stack preserves its original reading order.
