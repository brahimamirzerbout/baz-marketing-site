# Four Pricing Cards with Enterprise Option

Create a pricing section as one vertically stacked block inside a max-width container. At the top, place a centered heading on its own line; directly below the heading, a centered supporting paragraph at a comfortable reading width.

Below the heading block, place a horizontal row of four cards of equal width with consistent gutters between them. The first three cards are standard pricing cards; inside each, stack from top to bottom: a plan name on its own line; a short single-line description directly below the name; a large dominant price with a small "/month" suffix on the same baseline to the right of the amount; one full-width primary call-to-action button; and a vertical list of feature rows (each row composed of a small circular check icon at the left and a short feature label to its right on the same baseline).

The fourth card is an enterprise contact card whose composition differs: stack from top to bottom: a plan name on its own line; a longer descriptive paragraph (occupying the space where a price would normally sit) explaining the tier's scope; one full-width call-to-action button whose label reads as a contact prompt (e.g. "Contact sales"); and a short vertical list of high-level feature labels (each on its own line, without leading check icons).

On narrow viewports all four cards stack vertically in their original left-to-right order; each card stretches to the full content width with comfortable side padding.
