# Pricing Cards with Seat Slider

Create a pricing section as one vertically stacked block inside a max-width container. At the top, place a centered heading on its own line; directly below the heading, a centered supporting paragraph at a comfortable reading width.

Directly below the paragraph, place one wide control card spanning the full container width with rounded corners and a light border. Inside the control card, arrange a horizontal row containing: a short label on the left (e.g. "Number of seats"), a horizontal range slider track in the middle whose thumb position reflects the current value, and the current numeric value displayed in a small inline container on the right — all three elements sitting on the same baseline.

Below the control card, place a horizontal row of three pricing cards of equal width with consistent gutters between them. Inside each card, stack from top to bottom: a plan name on its own line; a short single-line description directly below the name; a large dominant price that updates dynamically with the slider value, paired with a small "/month" suffix on the same baseline to the right of the amount; one full-width primary call-to-action button; and a vertical list of feature rows (each row composed of a small circular check icon at the left and a short feature label to its right on the same baseline).

On narrow viewports the slider control card sits above the plan cards on its own line (the label, slider, and value may stack vertically within the card), and the three cards stack vertically in their original left-to-right order; each card stretches to the full content width with comfortable side padding.
