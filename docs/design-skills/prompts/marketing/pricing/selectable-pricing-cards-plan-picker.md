# Selectable Pricing Cards with Plan Picker

Create a pricing section split into two columns on large 1280 px width breakpoints inside a centered page container with comfortable side padding.

**Left column** is a left-aligned vertical stack containing, in order: a large left-aligned heading on its own line; a supporting paragraph directly below the heading in a muted body weight at a comfortable reading width; a short vertical list of three value- proposition rows (each row composed of a small tinted icon container at the left and a concise label to its right on the same baseline); and a social-proof row placed below the value list after extra vertical space, combining a tight horizontal cluster of overlapping small circular avatar thumbnails on the left with a single line of copy to their right containing a credible global usage count.

**Right column** contains a vertical stack of three full-width plan cards that behave as a radio group (exactly one plan is selected at a time). Each card is a rounded surface with generous padding. Inside each card, stack from top to bottom: a top row pairing the plan name (bold, left-aligned) with a circular radio indicator anchored in the top-right corner, and a single-line description directly below the name; a thin horizontal divider spanning the full card width; a dominant price line with a large numeric amount and a small "/month" suffix aligned on the same baseline to the right of the amount; and a short paragraph directly below the price summarizing what the plan includes (storage tiers, scope, or similar). Unselected cards use a plain surface with a hairline neutral border and an empty radio ring; the selected card uses a very light tinted background, a stronger accent border, a filled radio dot, and the price text rendered in the accent color.

Below the card stack, place a footer row spanning the right column width: on the left, a compact currency selector styled as a small dropdown (e.g. "$ USD" with a trailing chevron); on the right, two actions sitting side by side — a ghost or soft-outlined secondary button for terms (label may include a trailing arrow) and a solid primary button for checkout (label may include a trailing arrow), with the primary sized to its label rather than stretched full width.

On narrow viewports, stack the two columns vertically in reading order with the marketing block first and the selectable cards plus footer controls second; preserve tap targets and clear selected-versus-unselected visual states throughout.
