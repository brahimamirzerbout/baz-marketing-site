# Two-Column Customer Proof Section

Create a customer logos section split into two columns on large 1280px width breakpoints inside a centered page container with comfortable side padding.

The **left column** is a left-aligned vertical stack containing, in order from top to bottom: a prominent heading on its own line that may open with a large numeric or shorthand claim about client volume or reach; a supporting paragraph directly beneath the heading that contrasts or enumerates audiences served; a single horizontal band split into two equal columns where each column holds one stat block (each stat block stacks from top to bottom: a small pictogram, a concise one-line title directly below the pictogram, and one short supporting sentence beneath the title); and below the stat band, one full-width primary call-to-action control whose label invites deeper exploration of work or proof and may pair with a small leading icon to the left of the label text.

The **right column** contains only a two-by-two grid of four partner cells whose interior dividing rules (one vertical rule and one horizontal rule crossing at the center) make the block read as one tight quad. Inside each cell, stack from top to bottom: a centered partner mark; a single short partnership metadata line directly under the mark (for example how long the relationship has run); and at the bottom of the cell a compact "visit website" control whose label pairs with a small external-link affordance icon to the right.

On narrow viewports stack the columns with the left narrative-and-stats column first and the partner quad second; keep all internal stacks in their original reading order.
