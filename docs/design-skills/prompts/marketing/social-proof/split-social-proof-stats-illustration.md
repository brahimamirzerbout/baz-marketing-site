# Split Social Proof Stats With Illustration

Create a social proof section split into two columns on large 1280px width breakpoints inside a centered page container with comfortable side padding.

The **left column** is a left-aligned vertical stack containing, in order: a large left-aligned heading on its own line; a supporting paragraph directly beneath the heading at a comfortable reading width; a vertical list of three stat entries with consistent spacing between them — each entry is a horizontal row composed of a small rounded-square icon container anchored to the left (holding a single centered glyph) and a text block to the right of the icon containing a bold stat headline on its own line (combining a prominent numeric value with a short label, e.g. "500+ successful projects") and a short multi-line description paragraph directly beneath the headline. Below the stat list, after extra vertical space, place a social-proof row composed of a tight horizontal cluster of slightly overlapping small circular avatar thumbnails on the left and a single line of caption copy to their right that includes a numerically emphasized segment (e.g. a user count rendered in bold).

The **right column** is dominated by one large illustrative scene that spans most of the column area, vertically centered within the column. Allow simple supporting shapes (geometric accents or soft background forms) behind the scene if needed to fill the column without competing with the primary illustration.

On narrow viewports stack with the text column first and the illustration column directly below it; keep all internal stacks in their original reading order.
