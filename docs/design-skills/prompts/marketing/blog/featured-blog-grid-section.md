# Featured Blog Grid Section

Create a blog section as one vertically stacked block inside a max-width container with comfortable side padding. At the top, place a large centered heading on its own line (e.g. "Our Blog"); directly below the heading, a centered supporting paragraph of one to two lines at a comfortable reading width.

Below the header block, place a three-column grid on large breakpoints. The grid has three rows: the featured card in the left column spans all three rows in height, while the middle and right columns hold two standard cards each (filling four cells across two rows), plus a third row with one standard card in each of the middle and right columns — seven article cards total (one featured, six standard).

**Featured card** (left column, spanning all three rows in height) is a left-aligned vertical stack containing, in order: a theme-colored image placeholder box with a centered image icon and rounded corners spanning the full card width, occupying roughly the top half of the card; a bold multi-line headline rendered as a clickable link on its own line directly below the image; a short multi-line excerpt paragraph directly below the headline at a comfortable reading width; a compact outlined "View more" button with a trailing arrow icon pushed to the bottom of the card.

**Standard cards** (filling the middle and right columns, two per row across three rows) each stack from top to bottom: a bold headline rendered as a clickable link on its own line; a short two-to-three-line excerpt paragraph directly below the headline; a compact outlined "View more" button with a trailing arrow icon at the bottom of the card. These cards carry no image placeholder. Consistent vertical and horizontal gutters separate all cards.

On narrow viewports collapse the grid into a single column with the featured card first and the remaining six articles stacked beneath it in their original left-to-right, top-to-bottom reading order.
