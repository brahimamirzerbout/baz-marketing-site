# Centered Bordered Blog Card Grid

Create a blog section as one vertically stacked block inside a max-width container with comfortable side padding. At the top, place a large centered heading on its own line; directly below the heading, a centered supporting paragraph at a comfortable reading width.

Below the header block, place a three-column by two-row grid of six article cards with consistent gutters between all cards. Each card is a rounded panel with a thin outer boundary. Inside each card, stack from top to bottom: a theme-colored image placeholder box with a centered image icon and rounded corners spanning the full card width at the top; a small pill-shaped category badge composed of a tiny leading icon and a short label on its own line directly below the image; a bold multi-line headline rendered as a clickable link on its own line directly below the badge; a short multi-line excerpt paragraph directly below the headline; a bottom row composed of a small circular avatar on the left, the author name on its own line to the right of the avatar, and a publication date paired with a read-time label on a second line directly beneath the author name — both text lines left-aligned to the right of the avatar.

Below the entire card grid, centered on its own line, place a single text-style "View more" link composed of a short label paired with a trailing arrow icon.

On narrow viewports reduce the column count stepwise (three columns, then two, then one) until the cards form a single column on the narrowest breakpoints; preserve left-to-right top-to-bottom reading order throughout and keep the "View more" link centered below the card stack.
