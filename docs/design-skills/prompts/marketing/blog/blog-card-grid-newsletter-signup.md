# Blog Card Grid With Newsletter Signup

Create a blog section inside a centered page container with comfortable side padding. At the top, place a header row composed of a large left-aligned heading on the left and a compact pill-shaped "View more" link with a trailing arrow icon anchored to the right of the same baseline; directly below the heading, a left-aligned supporting paragraph at a comfortable reading width.

Below the header block, place a two-column by two-row grid of four article cards with consistent gutters between all cards. Each card is a rounded panel with a thin outer boundary and generous internal padding. Inside each card, stack from top to bottom: a top row composed of a small pill-shaped category badge with a tiny leading icon and short label on the left and a small time-ago indicator with a tiny clock icon and short label anchored to the right of the same baseline; a bold multi-line headline rendered as a clickable link on its own line directly below the top row; a short excerpt paragraph directly below the headline; a bottom row composed of a small circular avatar on the left, the author name and a publication date with read-time label stacked in two lines to the right of the avatar, and a text-style "Read more" link with a trailing arrow icon anchored to the far right of the same baseline.

Below the card grid, separated by generous vertical space, place a full-width newsletter signup block as a left-aligned vertical stack containing, in order: a bold sub-heading on its own line (e.g. "Sign up for our newsletter"); a short supporting paragraph directly below the sub-heading; a horizontal row composed of an email input field with a placeholder label and a small leading mail icon on the left and a compact primary "Subscribe" button immediately to the right of the input on the same baseline; a short line of fine-print copy directly below the input row referencing a privacy policy text link.

On narrow viewports collapse the card grid into a single column with the four cards stacked in their original reading order, and keep the newsletter block below them; preserve all internal stacks in their original reading order.
