# Centered Blog Card Grid

Create a blog section as one vertically stacked block inside a max-width container with comfortable side padding. At the top, place a large centered heading on its own line; directly below the heading, a centered supporting paragraph at a comfortable reading width.

Below the header block, place a three-column by two-row grid of six article cards with consistent gutters between all cards. Each card is a vertical stack containing, in order: a theme-colored image placeholder box with a centered image icon and rounded corners spanning the full card width at the top; a bold headline rendered as a clickable link on its own line directly below the image; a horizontal author row composed of a small circular avatar on the left, the author name to the right of the avatar, a small calendar icon and publication date to the right of the name — all on the same baseline directly below the headline; a short multi-line excerpt paragraph directly below the author row; a text-style "Read more" link composed of a small leading icon and a short label on its own line at the bottom of the card.

On narrow viewports reduce the column count stepwise (three columns, then two, then one) until the cards form a single column on the narrowest breakpoints; preserve left-to-right top-to-bottom reading order throughout.
