# Centered Blog Article Stack

Create a blog section as one centered vertical stack inside a max-width container with comfortable side padding. At the top, place a large centered heading on its own line (e.g. "Our Blog").

Below the heading, place a vertical stack of article entries separated by generous vertical space (no visible dividers between entries). Each entry is a centered vertical stack containing, in order: a small pill-shaped category badge composed of a tiny leading icon and a short category label, both centered horizontally on its own line; a large centered multi-line headline rendered as a clickable link on its own line directly below the badge; a centered author row on the same baseline composed of a small circular avatar on the left, the author name immediately to the right of the avatar, a small date icon and publication date label to the right of the name, a small views or shares icon and a numeric count to the right of the date — all elements sitting on a single horizontal line with subtle separators between them.

On narrow viewports keep the same single-column centered stack with all entries in their original reading order; allow the author row elements to wrap naturally while preserving logical grouping.
