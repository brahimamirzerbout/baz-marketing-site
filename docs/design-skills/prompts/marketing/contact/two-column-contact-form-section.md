# Two-Column Contact Form Section

Create a contact section split into two columns on large 1280px width breakpoints inside a centered page container with comfortable side padding. The two columns share the same vertical top alignment.

The **left column** is a left-aligned vertical stack containing, in order: a main heading on its own line; a supporting paragraph directly below the heading (one to three lines covering technical issues, product feedback, and business-plan questions); then three stacked contact blocks separated by comfortable vertical spacing. Each contact block is a small horizontal row at the top pairing a small leading icon (keyed to the block theme) at the left with a bold short block title to the right on the same baseline, followed by body lines directly beneath the title that sit flush-left under the icon-and-title row. The first block titles an office or regional brand name with a full multi-line postal address as body; the second block titles information and sales with a single mailto sales email address as body; the third block titles support with a short descriptive sentence and a mailto support email address as body.

The **right column** is a left-aligned vertical stack containing a form whose fields read top to bottom in this order: a labeled email input row (the label sits above the input on its own line and pairs a short label text with a tiny required-field marker to the right of the label; the input carries a leading envelope icon affordance flush-left inside the field and a placeholder suggesting a company email address); a labeled subject input row in the same shape directly below (the label pairs with an optional small circular question-mark information affordance to the right; the input carries a placeholder inviting a short subject line); a labeled multi-line message textarea directly below that (the label pairs with an optional small circular information affordance; the textarea carries a placeholder inviting the user to describe their inquiry); a single checkbox row containing a small square checkbox at the left and an inline label whose text states that submitting confirms reading and agreeing to the product terms of service (with the terms-of-service phrase rendered as an inline text link); one full-width primary submit button beneath the checkbox row whose label combines short send text and a trailing send-arrow glyph.

On narrow viewports, stack the two columns vertically with the contact-blocks column first and the form column second; keep all internal stacks in their original reading order.
