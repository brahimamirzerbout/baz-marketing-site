# Centered Contact Form Section

Create a contact section as a single centered vertical stack on a 640px width page background, constrained to a comfortable reading max width and centered horizontally with generous side padding.

At the very top, place a main page heading on its own line, centered horizontally. Directly below the heading, place a short supporting paragraph (one to two lines) that references technical issues, product feedback, and business-plan questions, also centered.

Below the supporting paragraph, stack a vertical form whose fields read top to bottom in this order: a labeled email input row (the label sits above the input on its own line and pairs a short label text with a tiny required-field marker to the right of the label; the input carries a leading envelope icon affordance flush-left inside the field and a placeholder suggesting a company email address); a labeled subject input row in the same shape directly below (the label sits above with a required-field marker; the input carries a leading pen or tag icon affordance and a placeholder inviting a short subject line); a labeled multi-line message textarea directly below that (the label sits above with a required-field marker and an optional small circular question-mark information affordance to the right of the label; the textarea carries a placeholder inviting the user to write their message and allows vertical expansion).

Finish the section with a horizontal flex row of two actions placed beneath the form, separated by a small inline gap: a secondary control at the left whose label combines a leading back-arrow glyph and short text that navigates back to the homepage; a primary control at the right whose label combines short send text and a trailing send-arrow glyph. Both controls sit on the same baseline and stretch to equal widths within the row.

On narrow viewports keep the same single-column stack, let the form occupy full content width with comfortable side padding, and stack the two action controls vertically (secondary above primary) if horizontal space is insufficient.
