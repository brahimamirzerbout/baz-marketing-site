# Team Showcase With Contact Strip

Create a contact-related page segment inside a centered page container constrained to a 1280px width with comfortable side padding. The segment is composed of two stacked zones: a team showcase zone at the top and a contact strip zone beneath it.

**Team showcase zone** is a single horizontal row (or wrapped grid) of member cards on wide breakpoints with equal card widths and comfortable gaps. Each card is a vertical stack containing, in order: a portrait media element at the top (square or slightly tall aspect ratio with rounded corners); an all-caps or emphasized name line on its own line directly below the portrait; a job-title line on its own line beneath the name; a short italic or quote-styled sentence about their craft on its own line beneath the title. On narrow viewports the row wraps into two columns or stacks into a single column.

**Contact strip zone** splits into two columns on large 1280px width breakpoints directly below the team showcase with generous top spacing.

The **left column** is a two-by-two grid of equal destination cells with small gaps between them. Each cell is a vertical stack containing a small ordinal numeral in one corner (top-left or top-right), a platform icon centered or near-centered in the cell, and a short platform-name label at the bottom. The four cells represent major social or chat channels (for example an image-oriented network, a professional network, a messenger app, and a chat app).

The **right column** is a left-aligned vertical stack containing, in order: a short imperative headline about working together on its own line; three single-line input fields stacked vertically (each with its label either above or rendered as a floating placeholder — for full name, phone number, and email address respectively); a single checkbox row containing a small square checkbox at the left and an inline label referencing agreement with a linked privacy policy (the privacy-policy phrase rendered as an inline text link); one full-width prominent submit button beneath the checkbox row whose label combines short send text and a trailing directional-arrow glyph.

On narrow viewports, stack the two columns vertically with the social grid first and the form column second; keep all internal stacks in their original reading order.
