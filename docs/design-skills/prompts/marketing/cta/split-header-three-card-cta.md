# Split Header Three-Card CTA

Create a CTA section in two vertically stacked parts inside a centered page container with comfortable side padding.

**Top part:** a two-column header row on large 1280 px width breakpoints. The left column contains a large heading on its own line and one short paragraph directly beneath the heading. The right column contains two longer paragraphs stacked one above the other, both left-aligned within the column. Align both columns to the top so the heading sits level with the first line of the right column's opening paragraph.

**Bottom part:** below the header row, leave generous vertical space, then place a three-column grid of equal-height cards on large 1280 px width breakpoints. Every card has softly rounded corners and a thin outer border.

The **first card** stacks from top to bottom: a medium heading on its own line; a short paragraph directly beneath the heading; a text link whose label pairs with a small external-link icon to the right of the link text (the link sits on its own line beneath the paragraph); a wrapping horizontal row of small pill-shaped chips where each chip pairs a tiny logo glyph at the left with a short product name to the right of the glyph (the chips wrap to additional lines if the row exceeds the card width).

The **second card** centers one tall phone mockup both horizontally and vertically within the card. The mockup's screen shows profile-style content (avatar, name, metadata lines) with a primary action button anchored to the bottom of the visible screen area inside the device frame.

The **third card** stacks from top to bottom: an extra-large numeric statistic on its own line (sized significantly larger than body text); a bold label line directly beneath the number describing the metric; a supporting paragraph directly beneath the label at a comfortable reading width.

On narrow viewports, stack the header columns vertically (left column first, right column second), then stack the three cards in a single column in their original left-to-right order.
