# Split 404 Page With Quick Links

Create a 404 page split into two columns on large 1280px width breakpoints inside a centered page container with comfortable side padding.

**Left column** is a left-aligned vertical stack, vertically centered within the column height, containing in order: a large multi-line heading stating the error status and a brief descriptive phrase on its own line; a supporting paragraph of one or two sentences directly below the heading at a comfortable reading width suggesting the user refresh or return to the homepage; a horizontal row of three compact card-style link blocks placed below the paragraph after extra vertical space, sitting side by side on the same baseline with consistent gutters between them. Each link block is a centered vertical stack composed of a small icon centered at the top (holding a single glyph) and a short label centered directly below the icon; the entire block behaves as a single clickable surface.

**Right column** holds one large illustrative scene that fills most of the column area, vertically centered within the column. The illustration depicts a figure or character in a questioning or searching pose alongside oversized question-mark shapes, conveying a lighthearted "lost" theme without relying on photographic imagery.

On narrow viewports stack the two columns vertically with the content column first and the illustration directly below it; keep all internal stacks in their original reading order with comfortable side padding.
